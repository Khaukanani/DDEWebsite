Deshun Deysel

Deshun Deysel is a pioneer, adventurer, entrepreneur, wife and mother. In addition to becoming the world’s first Black female high altitude mountaineer, Deshun’s determination has led her to running an international business (Deshun Deysel & Associates), which equips Business Leaders to Drive Agile Performance Development for todays’ ever-changing workplace. Among DDA’s clients are Fortune 500 companies and distruptive start-ups!

Deshun has inspired countless South Africans to set Big Scary Audacious goals and receives testimonials of their a accomplishments on a regular basis. She has addressed thousands of audiences worldwide - with her remarkable story of overcoming the limitations that the Apartheid regime has set for women of colour - to conquering the world of mountaineering and then spearheading new cognitive technologies in the world of business.

With over 20 years experience as an international speaker, moderator and business coach, Deshun adds incredible value to clients and helps them achieve conference objectives. She is consistently rated amongst the most impactful speakers both locally and internationally.

About Us

Aspiration, Self Mastery, Competence, Environment, Opportunity and Delivery are 6 of the major factors which drives performance in today's workplace. It also happens to be the Areas of Effectiveness which makes high altitude expeditions successful. By combining cutting edge research from organizations and big mountains, worldwide, DDA has formulated a digital platform called the Peak Performance Toolkit™ to equip teams with tools to Drive Agile Performance Development for career and business success.

Vision

To be the leading provider of Agile Performance Development solutions for 21st century workplace.

Mission

To equip bussiness leaders with the tools to Drive Agile Performance Development in a 21st century context.

Values

We endeavor to lead authentically, innovatively, and effectively in all that we do.

Purpose

We want to bring about real change and make a real difference in organisational productivity across the world.




Areas of Effectiveness

Based on our extensive research, we have identified that these 6 Areas of Effectiveness
create the key levers for individual, and thus, corporate success:




Aspiration

Values, Vission, Misssion and Purpose

Self-Mastery

Self Control, Self Efficacy, Motivation and Locus of Control

Competence

Talent, Skill, Knowlwdge and Experience

Environment

Me, Culture, Systems and Diversity

Opportunity

Preparations, Luck, Success and Failure,

Delivery

Decission Making, Delegating, Feedback and Implementation
